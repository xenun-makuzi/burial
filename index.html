Life Story

In Loving Memory

Harold James Whitfield, age 84, of Portland, Oregon, passed away peacefully on April 3, 2026, surrounded by his loving family. Born on March 14, 1942, in Eugene, Oregon, Harold spent his entire life dedicated to his family, his community, and his craft.

A graduate of the University of Oregon, Harold built a distinguished career as a civil engineer with the City of Portland for over 30 years. After retirement, he devoted himself to woodworking, creating intricate furniture pieces that now live in the homes of family and friends across the country.

Harold is survived by his beloved wife of 58 years, Dorothy; his three children, Michael (Sarah), Christine (David), and Robert; and six cherished grandchildren. He was preceded in death by his parents, James and Martha Whitfield.

What To Do When a Death Occurs

We know this is an overwhelming time. Here is a clear, step-by-step guide and we are available right now to walk you through everything.

If you need immediate help right now, please call us:

951 251 1474

Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week

Step-by-Step Guide

Your First Steps

1

Ensure Safety & Call for Help

If the death was sudden or unexpected, call 911 immediately. If the death occurred under hospice or medical supervision, call the attending nurse or hospice care provider. Do not move the body until authorized personnel arrive.

2

Notify Close Family & Loved Ones

Contact the immediate family members and those who were closest to the deceased. Allow yourself and others time to be present in this moment. There is no need to rush.

3

Call Eternal Rest Memorial

Call us at 951 251 1474 any time of day or night. Our funeral director on call will guide you through every next step, arrange for transportation of your loved one, and relieve you of the burden of coordination.

4

Locate Important Documents

When you are able, gather the following documents. Our team will let you know exactly which ones are needed and when — there is no immediate rush in most situations.

📋 Death certificate (issued by physician)
🆔 Government-issued ID
📄 Social Security card / number
📜 Will or trust documents
🏥 Insurance policies
🎖️ Military discharge papers (DD-214)
💳 Pre-arrangement records (if applicable)
🏦 Bank & financial account information
🧾 Recent tax returns
5

Notify Relevant Organizations

When you are ready, notify Social Security Administration, life insurance companies, the deceased's employer, banks and financial institutions, and any government benefit programs. Our team can provide guidance on each of these steps.

6

Plan the Arrangements

Our compassionate funeral directors will sit with you and your family to plan a service that truly honors your loved one. From burial or cremation to the personal touches that celebrate a life — we handle everything.
